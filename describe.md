# App Showcase Brief: "Reader"

Hi there! I'm the developer behind **Reader**, a premium, beautifully crafted read-later and RSS reader application built with Flutter. I've designed this app from the ground up to provide a serene, distraction-free environment for people to consume their favorite content across the web. I need you to build a stunning, modern application showcasing website that highlights the app's aesthetics, core features, and premium feel. 

Here is everything you need to know about the product:

## The Vision & Design Philosophy
I created Reader because I was tired of cluttered, overwhelming news feeds. The core design philosophy centers around a minimalist, premium reading experience. The app uses a highly curated, calming color palette (featuring bespoke colors like Clay, Mist Blue, and Sage) and fluid micro-animations (like subtle opacity changes and smooth navigation transitions). The typography is elegant, and the UI relies heavily on beautiful rounded cards, soft shadows, and clean layouts. The website should absolutely mirror this aesthetic: it must feel high-end, uncluttered, and deeply focused on typography and spacing. 

## Core Features to Highlight

### 1. The Reading Inbox
The heart of the app is the "Read" inbox. Instead of an overwhelming firehose of content, articles are elegantly listed with colored indicator dots to denote different RSS sources. Users can instantly tell read from unread articles with a simple visual cue. 
*   **Key Selling Point:** A tranquil, uncluttered feed that makes catching up on reading feel like a joy rather than a chore.

### 2. Intelligent Organization (Folders & Sources)
I've implemented a robust, Feedly-inspired folder organization system. Users can add their favorite RSS feeds and group them into custom folders. The UI features a slick, horizontally scrollable chip bar pinned to the top of the feed, allowing users to effortlessly filter their inbox by specific folders or individual sources with a single tap.
*   **Key Selling Point:** Complete control over your content. Group tech blogs in one folder, and news in another.

### 3. Swipe-to-Read Experience
This is one of the standout interactions. Beyond just scrolling through a list, I've built a dedicated swipe-friendly reader screen. Users can fluidly swipe through articles like flashcards, quickly marking them as read, saving them to their library, or diving in to read the full text. 
*   **Key Selling Point:** A highly tactile, engaging way to triage daily news. 

### 4. Personal Library & Highlights
Users aren't just reading; they're curating. Any article can be saved/bookmarked to a personal "Library." Furthermore, they can highlight specific text passages within an article and seamlessly share them via beautiful, stylized highlight cards to social media or messaging apps.
*   **Key Selling Point:** Never lose an important insight again. Save it, highlight it, and share it beautifully.

### 5. Smart Notifications
Users can configure exactly how they want to be notified about new content. Instead of spammy periodic alerts, the app offers a refined "cadence" setting (e.g., notifying the user 1 to 10 times a day) so they get a curated digest exactly when they prefer.

### 6. Premium Gateway
The app includes a "Premium Reader" tier. While I won't detail the pricing here, the website should definitely have a section subtly teasing the premium features, reinforcing the app's high-value proposition.

## Target Audience
Our users are discerning readers, professionals, researchers, and tech enthusiasts who value design, privacy, and control over their information diet. They appreciate good typography, dark modes, and software that respects their attention.

## Website Requirements (My Ask for You)
*   **Aesthetics:** Use a sleek dark mode or a very soft, paper-like light mode (matching our bespoke `Color(0xFFFFFCF5)` background). Use modern, clean typography (e.g., Inter or Outfit). Do not use aggressive, loud colors.
*   **Dynamic Elements:** Implement smooth scroll interactions, glassmorphism effects for navigation bars, and micro-animations on hover states to match the app's fluid feel.
*   **Hero Section:** A stunning phone mockup showing the "Read Inbox" or the "Swipe Reader" screen with a strong value proposition headline.
*   **Feature Scrollytelling:** As the user scrolls down, use alternating layouts or sticky scrolling to explain the Core Features (Inbox, Folders, Swipe-to-Read, Library). 

I hope this gives you a vivid picture of what I've built. Let's create a landing page that makes people instantly want to download it!
